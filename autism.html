+ Meditation for Autistic Children

Autism is no disorder. The autistic spectrum describes a neurological type. A nervous system prone to hypertension and overstimulation. Like almost all "disorders", it's merely a collection of related symptoms and not a description of any disease or cause of a disease. All human nervous systems are capable of autistic symptoms: obsession, urgent compulsion, panic, self-soothing, and rigid cognitive habits. All these symptoms are an expression of an overstimulated nervous system, not some mysterious "disorder". Autistic individuals (and I've met many) often possess extraordinary intelligence, intuition, and focus which can be utilized in extremely positive ways. As a software engineer, Theodore met many such individuals.

The medical attitude toward ASD (Autism Spectrum Disorder) is often that the odd behavior must be controlled, if not eliminated. People with ASD do often exhibit a typical panic and hypertension that can be difficult for everyone to live with. But rather than make an enemy of the uniqueness, it's possible to make friends with it and see where it can take us. Our instruction in meditation is designed to encourage the child to explore their ability to focus and control stimulation. The goal is not just that they "calm down", but to put their unusual minds to work - what these children can really do is as yet unknown.
